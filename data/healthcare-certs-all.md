# Tennessee CNA Certification - Complete Requirements Guide

## Overview

Certified Nursing Assistants (CNAs) in Tennessee provide essential basic patient care under the supervision of registered nurses and licensed practical nurses. This is an excellent entry-level position in healthcare with strong job prospects and opportunities for advancement.

## Basic Requirements

- **Age**: Must be at least 18 years old
- **Education**: High school diploma or GED required
- **Health**: Current TB test and physical examination
- **Background**: Clean criminal background check
- **Training**: Complete state-approved 75-hour training program

## Training Program Details

### Program Structure
Tennessee requires a minimum 75-hour training program that includes:
- **Classroom Instruction**: 16 hours of theory
- **Laboratory Practice**: Clinical skills development
- **Clinical Training**: 59 hours in actual healthcare facilities
- **Total Hours**: 75 hours minimum

### Training Duration
Most Tennessee CNA programs can be completed in:
- **Full-time**: 4-6 weeks
- **Part-time**: 8-12 weeks
- **Evening/Weekend**: 10-16 weeks

### Where to Train
Approved training programs are offered at:
- Tennessee Colleges of Applied Technology (TCATs)
- Community colleges
- Vocational schools
- Nursing homes and healthcare facilities
- Red Cross training centers

## Cost Breakdown

### Typical Program Costs
- **Tuition**: $400-$800
- **Books and Materials**: $50-$150
- **Uniform and Supplies**: $50-$100
- **Background Check**: $50-$75
- **State Exam Fee**: $100-$150
- **Total Range**: $650-$1,275

### Financial Aid Options
Tennessee offers several ways to cover CNA training costs:
- **TN Promise**: Free tuition at community colleges and TCATs for eligible students
- **TN Reconnect**: Free community college for adults 25+
- **Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)**: Funding for eligible individuals
- **Employer-Sponsored Training**: Many healthcare facilities offer free training

## Certification Process

### Step 1: Complete Training
Finish an approved 75-hour CNA training program

### Step 2: Submit Application
Apply to take the Tennessee Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation through Prometric

### Step 3: Take the Exam
The exam consists of two parts:
- **Written Test**: 60 multiple-choice questions (70 minutes)
- **Skills Test**: Demonstrate 5 randomly selected nursing skills

### Step 4: Passing Scores
- Written: Score of 70% or higher
- Skills: Satisfactorily perform all 5 skills
- Must pass both parts (can retake if needed)

### Step 5: Registry
Upon passing, you'll be added to the Tennessee Nurse Aide Registry

## Career Information

### Job Duties
Tennessee CNAs typically:
- Monitor and record vital signs
- Assist with daily living activities (bathing, dressing, eating)
- Help patients with mobility
- Provide emotional support
- Report changes in patient condition to nurses
- Maintain clean and safe patient environments

### Employment Settings
- Hospitals
- Nursing homes and long-term care facilities
- Assisted living facilities
- Home health agencies
- Rehabilitation centers
- Hospice care

### Salary Expectations
- **Entry Level**: $24,000-$28,000 per year
- **Experienced**: $28,000-$34,000 per year
- **Lead CNA**: $32,000-$38,000 per year
- **Hourly Range**: $12-$18 per hour

### Job Outlook
Tennessee has strong demand for CNAs with:
- Growing elderly population
- Expanding healthcare facilities
- High job placement rates (90%+)
- Opportunities for overtime and shift differentials

## Career Advancement

### Next Steps After CNA
- **Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN)**: 1-year program
- **Registered Nurse (RN)**: 2-4 year program
- **Medication Aide**: Additional certification
- **CNA Instructor**: With experience and additional training
- **Unit Secretary**: Administrative healthcare role

### Continuing Education
Tennessee CNAs must complete 12 hours of in-service training annually to maintain active registry status.

## Registry Maintenance

### Renewal Requirements
- Work at least 8 hours as a paid CNA within a 24-month period
- Complete required continuing education
- Maintain current contact information with the registry

### Reciprocity
Tennessee has reciprocity agreements with most states. CNAs certified in other states can typically transfer their certification to Tennessee with proper documentation.

### During Training

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long is my CNA certification valid?**
A: Your certification remains active as long as you work at least 8 hours as a paid CNA within every 24-month period.

**Q: Can I take the exam in Spanish?**
A: Yes, the written exam is available in Spanish and several other languages.

**Q: What if I fail the exam?**
A: You can retake the exam. There's a 30-day waiting period for the written test and no waiting period for the skills test.

**Q: Do I need a car for clinical training?**
A: Transportation to clinical sites is typically required. Some programs may offer transportation assistance.

**Q: Can I work as a CNA while in nursing school?**
A: Yes, many nursing students work as CNAs to gain experience and earn income while completing their RN degree.

- Requirements: High school diploma + training or experience
- Exam Cost: $155
- Renewal: Every 2 years

- Exam Cost: $90-$150
- Renewal: Annual

# Tennessee Promise Scholarship - Complete Guide

Tennessee Promise is a scholarship program that provides two years of **FREE tuition** at Tennessee community colleges and Tennessee Colleges of Applied Technology (TCATs) for high school graduates.

## Who Qualifies

- **Residency**: Tennessee resident
- **Status**: High school senior or recent graduate
- **Enrollment**: Attend Tennessee community college or TCAT
- **Continuous enrollment**: Enroll full-time or part-time, continuously
- **FAFSA**: Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)
- **Timeline**: Must be a continuous student (no gaps)

### Academic Requirements
- **GPA**: Maintain 2.0 cumulative GPA
- **Progress**: Meet Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) standards
- **Completion**: Complete 8 hours of community service per semester
- **Meetings**: Attend mandatory meetings with assigned mentor

- **Last-Dollar Scholarship**: Pays tuition and fees AFTER all other aid (Pell Grant, etc.)
- **Community Colleges**: All 13 Tennessee community colleges
- **TCATs**: All 27 Tennessee Colleges of Applied Technology

❌ Housing  
❌ Meals
